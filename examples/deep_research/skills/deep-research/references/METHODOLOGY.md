# Deep Research Methodology

## Research Quality Standards

### Source Evaluation Criteria

When evaluating sources, consider:

1. **Authority** - Is the source from a recognized expert or organization?
2. **Recency** - How recent is the information?
3. **Relevance** - Does it directly address the research question?
4. **Objectivity** - Is the source balanced or potentially biased?

### Source Diversity Matrix

Aim to cover at least 3 of these source types:

| Type | Examples | Weight |
|------|----------|--------|
| Primary | Official docs, papers, specs | High |
| Secondary | Analysis articles, reviews | Medium |
| Tertiary | Community discussions, forums | Low |

## Analysis Framework

### Theme Extraction Rules

- A theme must appear in at least 2 independent sources to be considered significant
- Themes should be specific enough to be actionable, not too broad
- Conflicting viewpoints on the same theme should both be noted

### Synthesis Guidelines

When synthesizing findings:

1. **Group by theme**, not by source
2. **Note consensus** - where do most sources agree?
3. **Highlight disagreements** - where do sources conflict?
4. **Identify gaps** - what questions remain unanswered?

## Report Quality Checklist

- [ ] Executive summary captures the most important findings
- [ ] Each claim is supported by at least one source
- [ ] Conflicting viewpoints are presented fairly
- [ ] Conclusions follow logically from the evidence
- [ ] Language is clear and accessible
- [ ] All sources are properly cited
